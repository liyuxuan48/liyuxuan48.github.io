Yuxuan Li

Ph.D. · University of California, Los Angeles
Project Engineer, Simerics Inc.

I obtained my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering at the University of California, Los Angeles in Dec. 2024. I graduated with a B.S. in Aeronautics and Astronautics from Shanghai Jiao Tong University in July 2019.

My research focuses on conjugate heat transfer modeling of oscillating heat pipes (OHPs), using immersed boundary methods and reduced-order modeling to study OHP dynamics, performance, and operation limits. My CFD experience spans compressible shock-bubble interactions to incompressible VoF ship hydrodynamics, with a shared focus on lightweight numerical methods for complex multiphysics problems.

Research Highlights

Oscillating heat pipe (OHP) low-order modeling — slug motions during an OHP start-up.

Immersed boundary methods (IBM) for heat transfer — temperature field with two heat sources and adiabatic boundaries.

RANS simulation of KRISO Container Ship (KCS) — wave-breaking characteristics.

Compressible flow simulation — late-time density evolution of shock-bubble interactions.
